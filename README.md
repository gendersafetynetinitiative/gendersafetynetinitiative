# Gender Safety Net

Gender Safety Net is a privacy-first, student-focused guidance tool designed to help students in Indian higher educational institutions understand their options when experiencing sexual harassment, gender-based discrimination, bullying, or hostile campus environments.

The tool provides anonymous, self-directed guidance through an interactive decision tree that helps students identify appropriate support pathways without collecting, storing, or transmitting any personal data. Users can explore formal reporting mechanisms, emotional support services, peer-support networks, and legal protections available under Indian law.

Key features include:

* Anonymous and privacy-preserving design with no data collection.
* Interactive guidance tailored to different experiences and support needs.
* Information on rights and protections under the POSH Act, 2013 and UGC Regulations, 2015.
* Clear explanation of what constitutes sexual harassment in educational settings.
* Guidance on Internal Complaints Committee (ICC) processes and timelines.
* Mental health and counselling resources for students.
* LGBTQ+ inclusive support resources and community organisations.
* Quick-exit functionality for user safety and privacy.

The tool is intended for educational and informational purposes only. It does not provide legal advice and does not initiate complaints or reports on behalf of users. All reporting decisions remain entirely with the student.

By combining legal information, support resources, and trauma-informed guidance, Gender Safety Net aims to lower barriers to help-seeking and empower students to make informed decisions about their wellbeing and safety.
